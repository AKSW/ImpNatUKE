INTRODUCTION  Previous work disclosed the presence in trunk bark of the  tree Ocotea catharinensis Mez (Lauraceae) of the neo-  lignans la, b, 2a, b, 3,4 and 5 [2]. Careful re-examination  of the extract led to the quantification  of the data and to  the additional  isolation of the neolignans 6a, b and &I  (Table 1). Relatively much smaller quantities of the same  compounds  (except ab), in addition to Zc, 7aA and 8b  were isolated from wood, while a considerable quantity of  7s was the sole neolignan located in leaves.
