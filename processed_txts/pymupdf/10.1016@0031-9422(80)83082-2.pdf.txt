INTRODUCTION  Iryanthera  laeuis  Markgr.  (Myristicaceae),  a tree which  may  attain  a height  of  25 m,  occurs  in  central  Amazonia  [2]. A sample of trunk wood of a specimen,  collected  in the vicinity  of Manaus,  yielded  besides  sitosterol,  7 flavonoids  which were classified  by trivial  ‘H NMR assignments  into dihydrochalcone  [3], flavan  [4] and 1,3-diarylpropane  [5] types.
