INTRODUCTION  Previous  publications  report the presence  of ellagic acid  and five derivatives  and of 7,3’,4’-trihydroxyflavone  and  three  derivatives  respectively  in the wood  of Erismu  calcaratum  (Link)  Warm.,  Salvertia  convullariodora  St.  Hil.. Vochysia acuminatu  Bongard,  1/: tyrsoidecr Pohl. [ 1 ]  and in the leaves of S. convalluriodora,  V cinnamomea  Pohl.,  K tucanorum  Mart. [2]. The present  communi-  cation  reports  the  presence  of  the  C-methylated  flavanones  la, 2a, 3a and benzophenone  4a in the wood  ofQu&a  labouriauana  Paula [3]. (2R)-5,7,4’-Trihydroxy-  3’-methoxy-6,8_dimethylflavanone  (la) was also isolated  from Q. paruensis Ducke [4], a further  South American  species of the Vochysiaceae.  RESULTS  Compounds  la and 2a are (2R)-flavanones  as shown by  ORD  curves [5] and the typical  NMR  signals  for the  protons  at C-2 and C-3 [6]. Due to the slight solubility of  la in the usual deuterated  solvents  the NMR  spectral  analysis  referred  to dimethyl  (1 b), diethyl  (Id)  and  dibenzyl  (le) ethers,  as well as to di-(lf)  and tri-(lg)  acetates.  Interpretation  of all signals led to the formula  C,,H,O,(OH),OMe.  Me,  for the natural  compound.  The presence of hydroxyls  at C-5 and C-7 was ascertained  by UV shifts, respectively  with AlCl, and NaOAc.  The  action of AICI, is not instantaneous,  a fact which, together  with  the  inertness  towards  etherification  and  even  acetylation,  indicates  the steric  hindrance  of the  S-  hydroxyl by the methyl group at C-6. The other methyl is  located at C-8 since the three' aromatic  protons,  forming  an ABX-system,  must  all be located  on ring B.
